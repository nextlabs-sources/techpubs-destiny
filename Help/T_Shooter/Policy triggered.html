Is this policy being triggered?

Once you know the enforcer is running, you should determine for sure whether the policy is being enforced but is unexpectedly returning an Allow result—and therefore is not producing any noticeable effect—or is not being enforced at all. To do this,

  1. Change the policy’s On Allow obligation to Log. This will create a record of every time the policy is enforced, even if the enforcement does not deny any action.

  2. After testing the policy a few times, use Reporter to run a policy activity report, filtering for just the policy you are troubleshooting. This will show whether the policy is being enforced or not. If it is, but is just giving you an unexpected behavior, most probably it is a policy design flaw, and you can skip to Inter-Policy Conflicts? If it is not being enforced, you need to check whether the policy is deployed at all on that enforcer--click Next.
